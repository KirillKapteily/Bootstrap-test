Microsoft Surface Go 4

Screen 14" IPS (1920x1200) WUXGA, Multi-touch, glossy / Intel Core i5-1135G7 (2.4 - 4.2 GHz) / RAM 16 GB / SSD 512 GB / Intel Iris Xe Graphics / without OD / Wi-Fi / Bluetooth / web - camera / without OS / 1.15 kg / black

Well for daily productivity Rating: ★★★★☆ (4/5) The Surface Go 4 runs well for daily productivity tasks like browsing, video calls, note-taking, and light document editing. The Intel Core i3 processor provides a noticeable boost compared to previous generations, but it's still best for moderate tasks rather than heavy multitasking. I use it for meetings and casual browsing, and it performs reliably without noticeable lag. The battery life lasts about 8 hours, which is fine for a workday, though power users may find themselves recharging mid-day. It’s perfect as a secondary device or a basic work tool, but heavy-duty users may need something with more power.

Portable and perfect Rating: ★★★★★ (5/5) The Microsoft Surface Go 4 is incredibly portable and perfect for anyone needing a lightweight device on the go. At under 550 grams, it’s one of the most compact tablets out there, making it easy to slip into a bag or carry around all day. The build quality is premium, with a solid magnesium body that feels durable despite its size. The kickstand is versatile, offering multiple viewing angles, which is great for both work and entertainment. If you prioritize mobility without sacrificing quality, the Surface Go 4 is fantastic.

Display Rating: ★★★☆☆ (3/5) The 10.5-inch PixelSense display on the Surface Go 4 is vibrant and sharp, ideal for reading, streaming, and sketching. It’s small but very clear, with responsive touch functionality and support for the Surface Pen, which is great for jotting down notes or doodling. However, the accessories, like the Surface Type Cover and Surface Pen, are sold separately, which adds to the overall cost. The Type Cover is almost essential for typing, and it works well, but I wish it were included at this price point. Overall, the Surface Go 4 is great if you’re willing to invest in the accessories, but keep in mind that it’s an added expense.
