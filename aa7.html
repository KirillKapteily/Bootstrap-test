Acer Aspire 7

Aspire 7 is a compact laptop with a high-quality Full HD IPS display and a rich set of interfaces. Thanks to the high-performance hardware, the laptop will perfectly cope with the most demanding tasks, and is also perfect for modern games on high settings (e.g. Dota 2, CS:GO, Fortnite, GTA V, etc.). Stylish minimalist case with a metal cover Intel Core i5-12450H processor (8 cores/12 threads) and NVIDIA GeForce RTX 2050 4 GB graphics card 16 GB of RAM and PCIe 512 GB SDD drive (maximum RAM is 32 GB) 15.6" FullHD display with IPS matrix HD webcam 720p Two built-in microphones Amber backlit keyboard Up to 8 hours of work without recharging

Excellent performance for the price Rating: ★★★★☆ (4/5) The Acer Aspire 7 offers excellent performance for the price, making it a solid choice for both productivity and light gaming. Equipped with the AMD Ryzen 5 processor and NVIDIA GTX 1650 graphics card, it handles multitasking smoothly and runs most modern games at medium settings without lag. I use it for video editing as well, and it processes tasks surprisingly fast for a laptop in this price range. The 512GB SSD is quick, and there’s an additional slot for more storage if needed. While the performance is great, the battery life could be better—it only lasts around 5 hours with regular use.

Nice laptopRating: ★★★★☆ (4/5) The 15.6-inch FHD display on the Acer Aspire 7 is decent but not outstanding. The colors are fairly accurate, but it could be a bit brighter, especially for use outdoors or in brightly lit rooms. The design is understated and professional, with a nice, matte finish that resists fingerprints. Build quality is mostly solid, though the plastic chassis feels a little flimsy compared to more premium laptops. The keyboard is comfortable and backlit, which is a great feature at this price. Overall, the Aspire 7 is well-built for everyday use, though there’s room for improvement in the screen brightness and materials.

Connectivity options Rating: ★★★★☆ (4/5) The Acer Aspire 7 provides a good range of connectivity options, including USB-C, multiple USB-A ports, HDMI, Ethernet, and an SD card reader, making it versatile for work and media transfer. Wi-Fi 6 support ensures faster internet speeds, which I’ve found very reliable, even when streaming. At around 2.1 kg, it’s a bit heavy to carry around all day but manageable if you need it for school or work. The weight and slightly larger profile might be a deal-breaker for those who prioritize portability, but as a desktop replacement or stationary laptop, it’s an excellent choice with great connectivity options.
